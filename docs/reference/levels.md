# PvZ1 Level and Map Reference

Comprehensive reference of all world types and level mechanics in Plants vs. Zombies (original/GOTY edition).

## Legend

- **Level-range** — Adventure mode levels (World-Level format)
- **Grid-layout** — Playable grid dimensions (rows × columns). All grids are 9 columns wide; rows vary by world.
- **Day/Night** — Lighting condition; affects mushroom sleep and sun generation
- **Special-mechanic** — The defining gameplay twist for this world type

## World Types

| World | Level-range | Grid-layout | Day/Night | Special-mechanic | Unique mechanics |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Day (Front Yard) | 1-1 to 1-10 | 5 rows × 9 cols (grass) | Day | None (tutorial world) | Standard lane-based play. Passive sun falls from the sky every ~10 seconds. All plants are available as unlocked. Serves as the teaching world — introduces one new plant per level. Simple zombie types only (no specials until 1-7). |
| Night (Front Yard) | 2-1 to 2-10 | 5 rows × 9 cols (grass) | Night | No passive sun; mushrooms awake; graves | No sun falls from the sky — economy relies entirely on Sunflower/Sun-shroom. Mushroom plants are free to place and don't require Coffee Bean. Graves appear in the rightmost ~3 columns and spawn zombies at the end of waves. Grave Buster can clear them. Fog-free night — full visibility. |
| Pool (Backyard) | 3-1 to 3-10 | 6 rows × 9 cols (4 grass + 2 water) | Day | Water lanes; Lily Pad required | Two centre rows are water lanes. Plants must be placed on Lily Pad to occupy water tiles. Aquatic plants (Tangle Kelp, Sea-shroom) can be planted directly in water. Zombies with Ducky Tubes swim across water lanes. Pool introduces Snorkel Zombie (dives underwater). The extra lane increases width pressure. |
| Fog (Backyard) | 4-1 to 4-10 | 6 rows × 9 cols (4 grass + 2 water) | Night + Fog | Fog obscures right half; water lanes | Same 6-row pool layout as Pool world, but at night with dense fog covering the right ~5 columns. Plantern clears fog in a radius; Blover temporarily clears all fog. No passive sun (night). Mushrooms are awake. Fog hides approaching zombies — first encounter is often too close for comfort. Combines water-lane pressure with visibility denial. |
| Roof (Rooftop) | 5-1 to 5-10 | 5 rows × 9 cols (sloped roof tiles) | Day | Flower Pot required; sloped trajectory | All plants must be placed on Flower Pot (roof tiles can't be planted directly). The roof slopes upward from left to right — straight-shooting plants in the leftmost columns fire peas that arc into the slope and stop short. Only lobber plants (Cabbage-pult, Kernel-pult, Melon-pult) reliably hit zombies across the full lane. Introduces Bungee Zombie (drops from above). Final level (5-10) is the Dr. Zomboss boss fight. |

## Additional Level Types (Mini-games, Puzzles, Survival)

Beyond the 5 Adventure worlds, PvZ1 includes several special level types that remix the core mechanics:

### Mini-games
| Level type | Description |
|---|---|
| Wall-nut Bowling | Plants are replaced with rolling Wall-nuts that bounce off zombies like bowling pins. No sun economy — Wall-nuts are provided on a conveyor belt. |
| Slot Machine | A slot machine determines which plants you receive. Match 3 sun symbols for bonus sun. Randomised loadout per spin. |
| Zombotany | Zombies have plant heads (Peashooter Zombie shoots back, Wall-nut Zombie is armoured). A role-reversal twist. |
| Whack a Zombie | Mallet-based mini-game — tap graves to whack emerging zombies. No planting, pure reflexes. |
| Last Stand | Start with 5000 sun and pre-place your entire defence. Survive 5 flag waves with no additional sun income. Strategic planning puzzle. |
| Bobsled Bonanza | Zomboni and Bobsled Team spam level. Ice trails everywhere — Jalapeno is essential. |
| Column Like You See 'Em | All zombies appear in pre-placed columns. Player must adapt quickly to mixed-type columns. |
| Pogo Party | All zombies are Pogo Zombies. Magnet-shroom or Tall-nut essential. |
| Dr. Zomboss's Revenge | Rematch with Dr. Zomboss at higher difficulty. Same mech mechanics but faster, more zombies. |

### Puzzle Modes
| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| Vasebreaker | Break vases to reveal random plants or zombies. Strategy: break vases near defences first. Randomised content per vase. |
| I, Zombie | Play as the zombie side — spend sun to deploy zombie types against pre-placed plant defences. Must eat all the brains. Role-reversal puzzle. |

### Survival Mode
| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| Survival: Day/Night/Pool/Fog/Roof | Play through multiple flag-waves in each world with persistent plant placement between rounds. Economy carries over. Endurance test. |
| Survival: Endless | Infinite waves on the Pool map. Difficulty escalates continuously — eventually multiple Gargantuars per wave. The ultimate PvZ challenge. Cob Cannon + Winter Melon + Gloom-shroom meta defines high-flag strategies. |

## Grid and Lane Mechanics

### Standard grid
- 9 columns (leftmost = home base, rightmost = zombie spawn)
- Column 1 is the lawn mower / pool cleaner column (last-resort defence)
- Zombies spawn from the right edge and walk left
- Each row is an independent lane — zombies don't cross lanes (except Dancing Zombie backup dancers)

### Water lanes (Pool and Fog worlds)
- Rows 3 and 4 (middle two) are water
- Require Lily Pad for non-aquatic plants
- Pool cleaners replace lawn mowers on water lanes
- Aquatic zombies: Ducky Tube variants, Snorkel Zombie, Dolphin Rider

### Roof slope
- Columns 1-5 are on the upward slope
- Straight-shooting plants in columns 1-4 have reduced effective range (peas hit the slope)
- Lobber plants are unaffected by slope
- No lawn mowers on the roof — replaced by nothing (no last-resort defence)
